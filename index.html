About


The purpose of Design Summer is to organize events for Bay Area interns interested in design. Over the course of a summer, we plan to organize lunches, tours, design thinking workshops, and expeditions to design practices and museums around the Bay Area. This diverse program will allow participants to interact with industry leading designers and gain exposure to the thriving design scene in San Francisco and its surroundings. Join us to meet other talented interns, discover new places, and learn from and inspire each other!


One summer. Infinite possibilities.

FAQ


What kinds of design are we talking about?
We've purposely avoided a more specified definition of design. Throughout the summer, we hope to explore a variety of design disciplines including graphic design, product design, and more. We're interested in discovering the different perspectives from which people approach their own design work.

Do you have a schedule of events?
As we get closer to the summer, we'll post a schedule of events here. Rest assured we're hard at work planning a summer full of workshops, talks, meals, and more. Stay tuned!

What if I'm not a current intern?
Because space at events is limited, we're only allowing current interns to participate in our activities. Sorry!
What am I signing up for?
We plan to organize lunches, meetups, workshops, and excursions to companies and museums in the Bay Area around the common theme of design as a way of thinking. You're signing up to be notified of when these events occur so that you can register for them.

Who are you?
We're two undergraduates in Computer Science at Princeton University who love design and are heavily involved in the design community on campus. Jonathan is an intern at Square and has previously worked with Coursera through the KPCB Fellows Program. He designs for Princeton Entrepreneurship Club and is interested in making things that bridge the digital and physical. Eric is the director of design for Princeton Entrepreneurship Club, the cofounder of Princeton University Letterpress, and dev lead for Princeton Innovation. He enjoys exploring generative design and design as a process.
